# User Story: [Managing Connections]

## Story Overview

**As a** Sereus Chat user with multiple connections,
**I want to** View all my connections and easily select who I want to chat with,
**So that** I can efficiently communicate with the right person without searching through clutter.

**Context:**
After using Sereus Chat for a few months, Bob now has connections with 15 people including family, friends, and coworkers.
Some conversations are very active, others haven't been touched in weeks.
Bob wants to message his coworker Sarah about a project but can't remember if they have a chat channel yet or if he needs to invite her.
Susan has 30+ connections and is getting overwhelmed trying to find specific people.

## Primary Sequence Path
1. Bob opens Sereus Chat to his main screen.
2. He sees a list of all his active chat connections.
3. The list shows each person's name and the most recent message preview.
4. He scrolls through the list looking for Sarah.
5. He doesn't see Sarah in his connections list.
6. He realizes he hasn't invited Sarah yet.
7. He generates a new invitation to send to Sarah.

**Alternative Path A: [Finding and Selecting Connection]**
4. Bob sees Sarah in his list.
5. He taps on Sarah's name.
6. The chat interface opens showing their conversation history.
7. He types and sends his message about the project.

**Alternative Path B: [Filtering by Name]**
1. Susan opens Sereus Chat and sees her long list of 30+ connections.
2. She uses the search/filter feature.
3. She types "Mar" to find her sister Martha.
4. The list immediately filters to show only matching names.
5. She sees Martha and two other connections with "Mar" in their names.
6. She selects Martha.
7. The chat opens and she sends her message.

**Alternative Path C: [Sorting Connections]**
2. Bob wants to focus on his most active conversations.
3. He sorts his connections by most recent activity.
4. His most active chats appear at the top.
4.1. He notices conversations with Mike and Susan at the top.
4.2. Old conversations with acquaintances are near the bottom.
5. He easily finds and opens his chat with Mike.

**Alternative Path D: [Viewing Connection Details]**
4. Bob finds Sarah but wants to verify it's the right Sarah before messaging.
5. He views Sarah's profile details.
6. He sees the profile information Sarah shared (name, email).
7. He confirms it's his coworker Sarah.
8. He opens the chat and messages her.

**Alternative Path E: [No Active Conversations]**
2. Bob sees that several connections exist but no messages have been exchanged yet.
3. These appear in his connections list with an indicator showing "No messages yet".
4. He selects one and initiates the first conversation.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Users see a list of all their connections when opening the app.
- [ ] Each connection shows the person's name and recent message preview.
- [ ] Users can search/filter connections by name.
- [ ] Users can sort connections (by recent activity, alphabetically, etc.).
- [ ] Users can view connection profile details.
- [ ] Users can easily distinguish between active conversations and new connections with no messages.
- [ ] Selecting a connection opens the chat interface.
- [ ] Users can identify when they need to create a new invitation for someone not in their list.
